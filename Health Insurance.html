Health Insurance Data Analysis with Python

Introduction

Analysis of Health checkup data of people collected for south Korea National Health Insurance. In Korea, everyone is compulsorily required to join the National Health Insurance. The National Health Insurance Service (NHIS), which manages national health insurance, provides basic health checkups to subscribers every year.
Collected dataset from kaggel-
Link - https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/hongseoi/health-checkup-result?select=2020.csv

Executive Summary

➢ This health insurance data analysis project aimed to explore and analyze a comprehensive dataset containing various health-related factors of individuals.
➢ The analysis focused on understanding the relationships between different variables and their potential impact on health insurance claims and risk assessment.
➢ This data analysis project sheds light on the crucial role of data analytics in the health insurance industry, offering actionable insights for informed decision-making and improved risk assessment practices.

Problem Statement

Health Profile by Age and Gender: Examine the health profiles of individuals by age and gender. Analyze various health parameters such as blood pressure, cholesterol levels, fasting blood sugar, and body mass index (BMI) across different age groups and genders. Identify any age or gender-related variations in health indicators and explore potential implications for health insurance and healthcare services.

Data Wrangling

➢ Performed comprehensive data wrangling tasks, including data cleaning, transformation, and merging, to ensure high data quality and consistency.
➢ Utilized Python libraries such as Pandas and NumPy to efficiently handle missing values, handle outliers, and reshape the data for analysis purposes.

Conclusion

➢ The analysis reveals a correlation between age and high blood pressure, emphasizing the importance of targeted interventions and regular monitoring for older age groups to address this prevalent health issue.
➢ The data highlights a higher prevalence of obesity among females and suggests a slight increase in obesity as age progresses, emphasizing the need to promote healthy lifestyles across all age groups.
➢ The analysis shows a higher prevalence of diabetes among males, with an increasing count as age advances, underscoring the significance of diabetes prevention and management strategies, particularly for older individuals.
➢ As age increases, there is a rising prevalence of high cholesterol, especially observed in the age group of 40. Early monitoring and lifestyle interventions are crucial to prevent cholesterol-related complications and manage cholesterol levels as individuals grow older.
